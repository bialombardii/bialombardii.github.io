Research

Master Thesis
Local Immigrant Presence and Candidate Composition: Evidence from Emilia-Romagna Municipal Elections +
Immigration Candidate composition Political inclusion Hand-collected data IV strategy

In my Master's thesis, I examine the relationship between local immigrant presence and the inclusion of migrant-origin candidates on electoral lists in Emilia-Romagna municipal elections between 2019 and 2024. By employing an original candidate-level dataset, I find a positive association between immigrant population share and migrant-origin candidacy. This relationship varies across list types and holds after isolating non-enfranchised immigrant populations, suggesting that candidate recruitment responds to demographic presence beyond direct electoral incentives.

University Project
Gender Equality and Immigration: Do Low-Skilled Immigrants Enhance Women's Participation in the Labor Force? +
Immigration Female labor supply Childcare IV strategy ACS data

In this group project, we examine how low-skilled immigration affects native women's labor supply in the US using American Community Survey data and an instrumental variable strategy. We find no significant effect at either the intensive or extensive margin for the full sample of high-skill native women. However, restricting the sample to women with children reveals a significant effect at the intensive margin, consistent with existing literature identifying childcare as the primary barrier to women's labor force participation. This work was produced for a course in Econometric Methods for Policy Evaluation taught by Professor Guglielmo Barone.

University Project
Feminine Strengths in STEM: The Power of Gender-Congruent Role Models +
Gender gap STEM enrollment Role models RCT Randomized saturation design

In this project, I design a randomized controlled trial aimed at increasing girls' university STEM enrollment through exposure to female role models. By implementing a randomized saturation design I address challenges arising from potential spillover effects between the two study groups. This work was produced for a Field Experiment class taught by Professor Denni Tommasi.

University Project
Expanding Union Participation Among Low-Skill Workers to Address Income Inequality in the US +
Unionization Wage inequality Low-skill workers CPS data Union wage premium

In this group project, together with Edi Maria Faraglia and Francesco Ballarin, we explore the relationship between unionization and wage distribution in the US using data from the Current Population Survey (2024). We find that while the union wage premium is higher for low-skill workers, these are also the ones displaying lower unionization rates, suggesting barriers to access rather than lack of benefit. This work was produced for a Labour Economics class taught by Professor Vincenzo Scrutinio.
